National Security

Defend the Homeland

Space situational awareness & planetary defense

  • The Minor Planet Center is the world's sole authority for tracking asteroids and comets — including potentially hazardous near-Earth objects — serving as the foundational data layer for NORAD, the U.S. Space Force, and NASA's Planetary Defense Coordination Office.
  • HITRAN, the molecular spectroscopy database, underpins all U.S. military atmospheric modeling and is depended on for directed energy, infrared sensing, and thermal imaging — with no commercial or government alternative.
  • The Greenland Telescope at Pituffik Space Base anchors a visible U.S. scientific presence at the military's northernmost installation.

AI & Open Science

Accelerate AI for Science

Frontier AI and open data infrastructure

  • AstroAI applies frontier AI to astrophysical datasets at scale, already partnering with the Department of Energy and NVIDIA on early AI-for-science initiatives.
  • The Astrophysics Data System and NASA SciX serve 16+ million unique visitors a year — the discovery engine for the world's scientific literature, open and machine-readable.
  • SAO publishes raw data and code in public repositories, exemplifying reproducible, transparent, merit-reviewed science.

Frontier Science

Secure American Leadership

Next-generation telescopes & technology

  • SAO is building critical Giant Magellan Telescope instrumentation — including a revolutionary spectrograph to search for life on other worlds — for a project backed by nearly $1 billion in private investment across 36 states.
  • SAO leads the world in black hole imaging through the Event Horizon Telescope, with direct technology transfer to defense and communications.
  • SAO operates the Submillimeter Array and Whipple Observatory on behalf of NASA and the entire U.S. astronomy community — infrastructure serving researchers, educators, and industry in all 50 states.

Workforce & Value

Maximize Taxpayer Value

Building America's STEM workforce

  • Central Engineering supports multiple missions simultaneously, avoiding the duplicative costs of fragmenting capabilities across project-specific contracts.
  • Smithsonian STARS, REU programs, and postdoctoral fellowships feed world-unique talent into the technical workforce — training tomorrow's mission PIs and industry leaders for American aerospace and defense.
  1. 01

    Humanity's first image of a black hole

    The Event Horizon Telescope, led from SAO, unveiled the glowing ring of M87* in 2019 — then our own galaxy's Sagittarius A* in 2022 — turning an untestable idea into an observable object.

  2. 02

    The accelerating universe

    The High-Z Supernova Search, co-founded at the CfA, discovered that cosmic expansion is speeding up — revealing dark energy and earning the 2011 Nobel Prize in Physics.

  3. 03

    Direct evidence for dark matter

    Chandra's image of the Bullet Cluster caught dark matter sailing ahead of colliding gas — the first direct empirical proof that it exists.

  4. 04

    The cosmic web

    The CfA Redshift Survey produced the first true maps of large-scale structure, discovering the "Great Wall" of galaxies and revealing that the universe is built of filaments and voids.

  5. 05

    The first transiting exoplanet — and the first alien atmosphere

    CfA astronomers watched HD 209458 b cross its star in 1999, then detected sodium in its atmosphere — founding the science of exoplanet characterization.

  6. 06

    Comets are icy worlds

    SAO director Fred Whipple's 1950 "dirty snowball" model explained what comets actually are — and guided every comet mission since.

  7. 07

    Touching the Sun

    SAO-built instruments aboard Parker Solar Probe counted the solar wind as the spacecraft crossed into the Sun's corona in 2021 — the first time humanity ever touched a star.

  8. 08

    Opening the X-ray universe

    From the Einstein Observatory to Chandra, SAO scientists built the field of X-ray astronomy — recognized with the 2002 Nobel Prize in Physics — and revealed the hot, violent cosmos.

  9. 09

    The fourth test of general relativity

    Irwin Shapiro, longtime SAO director, predicted and measured the time delay of radar signals grazing the Sun — a fundamental test of Einstein's theory that now bears his name.

  10. 10

    Weighing and shaping the Earth

    At the dawn of the Space Age, SAO's worldwide satellite-tracking network pioneered space geodesy — refining Earth's true shape and gravity field, and laying groundwork for modern GPS.

Where we come from

Over a century of looking up

SAO began on the National Mall with one man measuring sunlight — and grew into one of the largest astrophysics institutions on Earth.

1890

Born at the Smithsonian

Secretary Samuel Pierpont Langley founds the Smithsonian Astrophysical Observatory in a wooden shed behind the Castle in Washington, D.C., to study the Sun's energy — the "astro-physics" of its day.

1900s–1940s

Chasing the solar constant

Under Charles Greeley Abbot, SAO builds solar-monitoring stations on mountaintops from Chile to California, making decades of measurements of how the Sun powers Earth's climate.

1955

The move to Cambridge

SAO relocates to Cambridge, Massachusetts, alongside Harvard College Observatory — and within two years is tracking the first artificial satellites at the dawn of the Space Age.

1973

A powerful partnership

SAO and Harvard College Observatory join forces as the Center for Astrophysics — uniting Smithsonian and Harvard scientists in a single research community.

1999

Chandra opens the X-ray sky

NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory launches aboard Space Shuttle Columbia, with science operations led by SAO — and rewrites what we know about the high-energy universe.

2019

Seeing the unseeable

The Event Horizon Telescope, with SAO leadership, unveils the first image of a black hole — the glowing ring of M87*, seen around the world within hours.

Today

From Earth's air to cosmic dawn

TEMPO watches North America's air quality from orbit, the Giant Magellan Telescope rises in Chile, and SAO scientists probe everything from exoplanets to the first galaxies.
